Our Space

Sixty covers.
One experience.

Aubergine occupies a 19th-century townhouse in the West Village, its dining room a study in restrained elegance — pale oak, aged bronze, and linen that remembers what tablecloths used to mean.

We keep sixty covers because we believe that is the number at which a kitchen can still whisper rather than shout. Every service is a performance with a cast that knows the audience by name.

The Philosophy

Radical
seasonality.

Our menu changes completely with the seasons — not as a marketing note, but because we source exclusively from eight farms within two hundred miles of the restaurant. When the last of the autumn chestnuts are gone, they are gone.

This constraint is our greatest creative freedom. It forces invention rather than repetition, and it connects every dish to the landscape that made it possible.

The menu.

All menus are composed around what is finest at this exact moment. Dietary requirements are accommodated with pleasure — please inform us at the time of reservation.

I
Amuse-Bouche

A single bite: compressed cucumber with Osetra caviar, crème fraîche, and a frozen disc of dill oil. The first signal of the kitchen's intention.

II
Oyster & Sea

Kumamoto oyster, sea urchin emulsion, pickled green apple, frozen horseradish snow. Served at 2°C.

III
Chestnut & Truffle

Chestnut velouté poured tableside over a porcelain cup of shaved black truffle and toasted brioche croutons. Autumn's farewell.

IV
Diver Scallop

Maine diver scallop, celery root in three preparations, verjus beurre blanc, micro herbs from our kitchen garden.

V
Duck Confit

48-hour Moulard duck leg, fermented black garlic, sunchoke purée, pomegranate reduction, duck jus aged thirty days.

VI
A5 Wagyu

Four ounces of Miyazaki A5 striploin, charred leek ash butter, bone marrow soufflé, Périgueux sauce. The quiet centre of the evening.

VII–IX
Cheese & Dessert

Curated affinage cart from Murray's, followed by a pre-dessert of citrus sorbet, and a composed dessert of Valrhona chocolate with smoked salt caramel.

X
Mignardises

A selection of house-made confections: pâte de fruit, salted caramel bonbons, and a single madeleine, warm from the oven.

The Kitchen

Élise
Marchand

Executive Chef & Partner

Élise trained under Alain Passard at L'Arpège and Heston Blumenthal at The Fat Duck before returning to New York — where she grew up — to open Aubergine in 2016. Her cooking is rooted in classical technique but refuses its conservatism.

She writes a seasonal forager's journal, works closely with six Hudson Valley farms, and believes that the most important thing a chef can do is remain curious about the ingredients themselves rather than the impression they create.
